# Vowel Reduction

## What is vowel reduction?

It is common feature of spoken English. When a full vowel in a syllable or a word gets reduced to a schaw /ǝ/- or sometimes an /ɪ/.

这是与“元音弱化”（vowel reduction）相对的概念，指在重读音节或强读位置中，元音保持其原本清晰、完整的发音，未被弱化成/ə/或其他弱化元音。

> 专业背景补充：

- 在英语中，像 cat 里的 /æ/，bed 里的 /e/，都是完全元音。
- 而在非重读音节中（如 sofa 的第二个元音，effect 的第一个元音），这些元音通常会被弱化成中央元音 /ə/，这个过程就是元音弱化

## What’s the schwa /ǝ/ again?

It is the mid-central vowel sound. The most common sound of English!
E.g. banana /bǝ’na:nǝ/
What are examples?

| word | Full form | Vowel reduction / weak form |
| :--- | :-------- | :-------------------------- |
| Have | /hæv/     | /həv/ /əv/                  |
| Do   | /du:/     | /də/                        |
| Was  | /wɒz/     | /wəz/                       |

## What is a weak form?

A weak form is an unstressed word with a reduced vowel sound - a schwa. It is the result of natural , connected speech in English.

弱读形式是指单词在非重读情况下，元音发生弱化（通常弱读为中央元音/ə/）的发音现象。它是英语自然语流中连读的必然结果。

## Why does all of this happen?

English is an stress-timed language. There is stress on words = lexical (词汇的)stress, and on phrases and sentences=prosodic /prəʊ'sɒdɪk/ stress. （adj. 诗体学的；作诗法的；韵律学的）

Important words and syllables are stressed, others are not.
Unstressed words and syllables often experience vowel reduction because we say them faster.

Examples:
It was nice and warm but not interesting!

Full form vs. weak form
Sometimes a word is stressed and pronounced in a full form.
Sometimes it is unstressed and turns into a weak form.
Examples: he won a car and a house in the competition !here, ‘and’ is stressed and in its full form : /ænd/

Jim and liz are coming over for dinner later. Here , ‘and’ is unstressed and in its weak form :/ǝn/
Yes, we even lost the “d” - that is called elision!

Words affected by vowel reduction:

- Function words (at, then…) 前置词、连词等功能词
- Auxiliary verb (be, do, have) 助动词
- Modal verb (can, would…)
- Articles (the ,a )
